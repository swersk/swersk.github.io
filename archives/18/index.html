The truth behind Starbucks fraps

<p>I used to drink Starbucks frappuccinos like they were going out of style.  I'd have them all the time as a snack because I thought orering "lite" meant it was healthy. Little did I know that the ingredients used in the syrup are actually pretty dangerous. Ingredients of the base (not including sugar &amp; whipped cream):</p><p></p><p><strong>Light Coffee Frappuccino Syrup (US version): </strong>Water, sugar, Erythritol (E968), <em><span style="color:#ff0000;">Natural Flavors</span></em>, Salt, <em><span style="color:#ff0000;">Carageenan (E407), Xanthan Gum (E415), Maltodextrin, Preservative: Potassium Sorbate (E202), Citric Acid (E330), Reb A, Color: Caramel (E150d, E150b)</span></em></p><p></p><p><strong>Coffee Frappuccino Syrup: </strong>Sugar, Water, <em><span style="color:#ff0000;">Natural and Artificial Flavor, Xanthan Gum (E415), Preservative: Potassium Sorbate (E202), Citric Acid (E330), Caramel Color (E150)</span></em></p><p></p><p><span style="color:#000000;">Red= not good for you. Specifically:</span></p><p></p><p>Xantham gum: An industrial processed product/thickener/emulsifier that can cause bloating.</p><p></p><p>Caramel color: Used to add coloring, but doesn't add taste or nutritional value. The UN categorizes caramel color into 4 classes; the one found in Starbucks' frap is Class IV, the most dangerous kind. It is made by heating ammonia and sulfites under high pressure, creating a carcinogenic (re: cancerous) compound- specifically, lung, liver, and thyroid cancer. Coke and Pepsi both changed their formulas &amp; removed caramel color when thousands of cancer deaths were linked to the consumption of their sodas.  It's also banned in California. It's also found in Starbucks' "nutritious" Oatmeal. Having this on the FDA's list of approved additives totally delegitimizes the FDA, in my opinion, but that's a story for another day.</p><p></p><p>Fraps also have insane amounts of sugar- up to 78g!</p><p></p><p>I haven't had a Starbucks frap since I learned about this. The beauty is that by avoiding these seriously unhealthy foods, your body goes back to its equilibrium weight. It's a win-win.</p><p></p><p>[sources: Yani Hari &amp; Cristina Goyanes @ Livestrong]</p>
