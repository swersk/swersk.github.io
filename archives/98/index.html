Orange Juice: healthy or not so healthy?

<p><p>I was talking nutrition with a friend over dinner last night. The conversation started with something like this:</p><p>Him: I don't understand why they serve half sized beers in Europe.</p><p>Me: They're actually regular sized beers, it's just our beers are double the size.</p><p>{interesting how the food industry has warped our perception of appropriate portion sizes]</p><p>--2 minutes later--</p><p>Him: I try to be healthy. I don't have sodas or juices. Except orange juice, because that's healthy.</p><p> </p><p>The conversation stopped there, but I was tempted to continue. Is orange juice really healthy?</p><p>Some facts about OJ juice:</p><p>1) The label might say "100% orange juice" or "100% from concentrate", but this can be factually untrue. </p><p> </p><p>2) It's pasteurized, meaning it's boiled, de-areated, and stored for up to a year.
