Small Changes go a Long Way

Not only is it perfect on a cold day, but it's yummy and healthy.</p><p></p><p>I feel obliged to discuss my "inspirational conversations," in the hope that you'll be inspired too!</p><p></p><p>1) I was out to dinner with two Chinese friends who both ordered hot water before the meal. I thought it was a bit strange but intriguing. They told me all about the tea and hot-water-with-lemon culture in China; their culture believes in its ability to improve health by preventing sickness, increasing energy, and improving your skin.</p><p></p><p>2) My neuroscience/education professor told our class that it's never too late to learn a new language. In fact, he said, it's never too late to learn anything. You might not learn it as quickly, but with persistence and practice you'll pick it up. While drinking hot water with lemon isn't technically a new skill, I like to think of it in the framework as "I'm not too old to acquire a new habit."</p><p></p><p>Upon further research I learned that drinking water with lemon before meals is a great weight-loss tool because the citric acid contained in the lemon helps to activate digestive enzymes and hormones associated with satiation triggers. It activates chemical signals to your brain that says "enough is enough" while eating - the complete opposite of processed and refined foods like white bread/rice, snacks, fast foods, etc. that trick your brain into thinking you're hungry.</p><p></p><p>If you're afraid of the high acidity contained in lemons, just a small piece will do! Also, it's more effective if done on an empty stomach, like early in the morning or before a meal.</p><p></p><p>So in the spirit of new habits, I'm going to do some work over a hot cup of water with lemon and wait for the magic to happen! Did I mention it is also directly correlated with good grades? (I wish!)</p>
