Sugar: An intro

<p>Sugar seems to be all over the news these days. From NPR* to ABC News**, who claimed that bananas were officially deemed too high in sugar for monkeys- yes, monkeys -it seems like you can't read about nutrition without reading about sugar. What's the deal- is it just a transient hype? Another substance to target in our quest to lose weight? Or is this for realz?</p><p></p><p>My food passion and exploratory journey into nutrition actually began about 7 months after moving to Spain, in April 2011, when I realized that I no longer craved sugar. Ever. "That's happening to me too! And it happened to my sister here!" my Canadian friend told me, and I was immediately curious- what's driving this, or is it completely coincidence?</p><p></p><p>It wasn't until almost 2 years later when I read David Gillespie's "Sweet Poison" book on sugar that I realized there was a science behind it all: the less sugar you have, the less sugar you crave. Of course, it's more complicated than that (stay tuned!) Having been in an environment with less sugar additives, I simply stopped craving it- and lost some pounds along the way.</p><p></p><p>To get started, take this quiz to see if you're addicted to sugar. It's a little silly...but I'd say important questions to ask yourself are: Do you think about sugar or dessert even if you're not hungry? Do you 'crash' at 3pm every day and look for something sweet to eat or drink to help?
