Teach for America

I joined Teach for America because I wanted to change the trajectory of kids' lives. I ended up changing the trajectory of my own. As a Teach for America corps member in Washington Heights, New York City, I not only learned a lot about effective teaching, but I also learned how to adapt to unforeseen challenges, think creatively to solve tough problems, and lead a diverse group towards a common goal. Many of the challenges I encountered—grossly oversized classrooms, behind grade-level students, language barriers—required patience, resourcefulness, grit, investing others in my goals, and collaboration. Seeing the success of my students firsthand reaffirmed my belief that the achievement gap is a solvable problem, and I am just as committed, now as I was on day one, to make sure this becomes a reality.
