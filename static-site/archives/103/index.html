Yogurt: the good, the bad, and the ugly

Nonfat! 2x the protein! This yogurt is laden with smart marketing. <strong>But don't be fooled!</strong></p><p></p><p>Yogurt is one of those foods we automatically think is healthy. When I saw my friend eating this thinking she was having a healthy snack, I knew I had to snap some pics and inform the world that the yogurt you're probably eating is deceivingly bad for you.</p><p></p><p><strong>Check out the nutrition label:</strong></p><p>7g of sugar--&gt;that's 1/3 of your daily intake (for women, who should have about 20g/day)</p><p>0g fiber--&gt; if you're going to have sugar, at least have some fiber with it, like whole grain breads</p><p></p><p><strong>Ingredients list:</strong></p><p>Fructose--&gt;2nd listed ingredient. Ingredients are listed in descending order of predominance, so fructose is one of the main ingredients here. Fructose stimulates the release of a chemical in the brain that inhibits your ability to feel full, so eating fructose is like eating empty calories. When I read something has fructose, I imagine is having a label saying, "Eat me! You'll be hungry ten minutes later!"</p><p>Other scientific words--&gt; red flag</p><p><h2><strong>So what types of yogurts are good?</strong></h2></p><p>If you're looking for a healthy, protein-heavy snack, try a certified organic brand Greek yogurt, which should only have two ingredients: milk and active live cultures (also listed as Cultured Pasteurized Grade A Milk). Avoid yogurts with flavor or additives, including milk protein concentrate, gelatin kosher (a hidden source of MSG), whey protein concentrate, milk protein concentrate, organic corn starch.</p><p></p><p>Most of yogurt is made from milk, so choose a yogurt where the milk is from pastured cows that are NOT given antibiotics or growth hormones. Chiobani was recently taken off the shelves of Whole Foods because the milk they use contains GMOs (the cows feed is derived from genetically engineered crops, like corn and soy beans).</p><p></p><p>If you don't feel like looking through the ingredients, a quick rule of thumb is to avoid flavored yogurts. The "natural flavors" are manufactured using sketchy products- including wood pulp or macerated castor sac scent gland of a beaver (yes, the animal). Flavors also mean lots of unnecessary added sugar.</p><p><h2><strong>Summary of what to buy:</strong></h2></p><p>1) certified organic brand Greek yogurts. This ensures the yogurt is made from milk without antibiotics, growth hormones, or GMO feed.</p><p>2) plain yogurt, even if it's organic Greek yogurt.</p><p>3) yogurt with only 2 ingredients (milk + live active cultures). If you want, put some of your own fruit or nuts on top to make it more delicious.</p><p></p><p>My mom and I spent a solid 15-20 minutes in the supermarket looking for a yogurt that fits the criteria; it's hard, I admit, but once you find the right brand, you won't have to go through the arduous decision-making process again. Don't be lazy, it's worth it for your health(y body + mind)!</p><p></p><p>Sources: http://www.foodrenegade.com/, Non-GMO Project</p>
