Not all frozen foods are created equal

<p>Most of my friends eat frozen foods and think they're being healthy. If it's from Trader Joe's, it must be healthy, right? Nope.</p><p></p><p>I've been tempted to say that all frozen foods are bad. But upon more research, I realized that would be misleading. Here's the deal, yo.</p><p></p><p>When you're shopping for frozen foods, ignore the labels. Look for the sugar content and ingredients. Example:</p><p></p><p>Kashi Mayan Harvest Bake</p><p></p><p><img class="irc_mut" id="irc_mi" style="line-height:1.5em;margin-top:51px;" alt="" src="http://images.teamsugar.com/files/upl2/1/12981/07_2009/055d3f6f7f857098_mayan.jpg" width="334" height="292" /></p><p></p><p>All natural, 9g protein, 8g fiber...looks delicious AND healthy! I always get suspicious when they make health claims, though. Real foods shouldn't have to sell themselves. Now let's check the nutrition label:</p><p></p><p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3543" title="Plantain.Kashi" alt="" src="http://www.nutritiouseats.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/Plantain.Kashi_.jpg" width="330" height="980" /></p><p></p><p><strong>19g of sugar?!</strong> That's about 5 teaspoons of sugar. Women are supposed to have no more than 6 teaspoons/day, men 9 teaspoons/day. Fun fact: type in "how many teaspoons of sugar daily" into the google search box and it'll pop right up.</p><p></p><p>And for the ingredients, where do I even start?!</p>
