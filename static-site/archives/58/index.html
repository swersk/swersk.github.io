They say it doesn't feel like work when you're doing something you believe in. That's why I enjoyed working in partnership with <a title="UNICEF" href="http://www.unicef.org/">UNICEF</a> to advise on China's innovative K-12 education program on social and emotional learning (SEL).More about the project:<ul> <li><a href="https://laurenswersky.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/social-and-emotional-learning-programs-in-implementation1.pdf">Social and Emotional Learning Programs</a>: A literature review on social and emotional learning curricula and programs.</li> <li><a href="https://laurenswersky.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/p-a-t-h-s-to-adulthood-a-case-study.pdf">P.A.T.H.S to Adulthood- A Case Study</a>: A case study of Hong Kong's P.A.T.H.S SEL program that attempts to develop "21st century skills" using Pellegrino and Hilton's framework. Included is a proposal on how to effectively evaluate the program.</li> <li><a href="https://laurenswersky.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/transferring-project-p-a-t-h-s-to-mainland-china-small-adjustments-large-gains.pdf">Transferring Project P.A.T.H.S. to Mainland China- Small Adjustments, Large Gains</a>: An exploration of the relevancy and feasibility of transferring SEL programs to mainland China as aligned with UNICEF's education strategy.</li> <li><a href="https://laurenswersky.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/unicef-vs-world-bank.docx">UNICEF vs. World Bank</a>: A synthesis of the comparative education strategies of UNICEF and the World Bank.</li></ul>
