Can beer be healthy?

<p>Good news: Being healthy doesn't mean you have to avoid beer! It just means choosing the right ones- the ones without toxic and genetically modified ingredients that make us sick and perpetuate our sugar cravings.</p><p></p><p>Fun fact: Beer is regulated by the Department of Treasury, not the Food and Drug Administration, which means listing ingredients is voluntary and guidelines overall are lax. That's why you don't see calories or percent alcohol listed on beers (except light ones).</p><p></p><p>Many beers contain additives, including added sugars like high fructose corn syrup, caramel coloring, MSG, and calcium disodium EDTA containing formaldehyde, and more...gross.</p><p></p><p>But have no fear! From my research, I found these to be the <strong>purest, chemical-free beers</strong>:</p><p></p><p>Amstel Light, Heineken, and Sierra Nevada</p><p></p><p><strong>and</strong></p><p></p><p>locally made beer from microbreweries, which are more likely to produce pure beer</p><p></p><p><strong>and </strong></p><p></p><p>organic beer.</p><p></p><p><strong>Avoid these</strong>: Budweiser, Budlight and Michelob Ultra (contain dextrose); Corona, Foster's, Miller Light, and Pabst (contain corn syrup); Newcastle (artificial caramel color that is banned in other countries).</p><p></p><p>This is a gross simplification of beer and its ingredients, but the overall take-away from my research was that most beers contain GMOs, and that we should all feel confident having Heineken, Sierra Nevada, and Amstel Light as our go-to beers. (I promise they didn't pay me off to say that- though that would make for an interesting story!)</p><p></p><p>Sources: http://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s002170050478, http://www.care2.com/greenliving/toxic-and-gmo-ingredients-in-your-beer.html, Foodbabe</p>
