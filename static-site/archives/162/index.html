It's all over by the time you're 5

<p><p>I'll never forget when my economics professor claimed that, "It's all over by the time you're age 5." While he was referring to return on investments of early childhood education, the comment can also apply to childhood obesity.</p><p>According to new study released this week, a child who enters kindergarten overweight has a 32% chance of becoming obese, 4 times the chance of normal-weight children. By the time a child enters kindergarten, much of the risk factors are already in place. In other words, if you make it to kindergarten without being heavy, your chances of being a normal weight for life are significantly better.</p><p>This study is a reminder of how important the first 5 years of life are. We must address obesity from a developmental standpoint and prevention efforts must begin much earlier than we originally thought.</p><p>Source:Â http://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMoa1309753?query=featured_home&amp;</p></p>
