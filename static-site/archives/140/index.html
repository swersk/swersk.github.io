Teach for America

<p>I joined Teach for America because I wanted to&nbsp;change the trajectory of kids' lives. I&nbsp;ended up&nbsp;changing the&nbsp;trajectory of&nbsp;my own.</p><p></p><p>As a Teach for America corps member in Washington Heights, New York City, I not only learned a lot about effective teaching, but I also learned how to adapt to unforeseen challenges, think creatively to solve tough problems, and lead a diverse group towards a common goal.</p><p></p><p>Many of the challenges&nbsp;I encountered—grossly oversized classrooms, behind grade-level&nbsp;students, language barriers—required patience, resourcefulness, grit, investing others in my goals, and collaboration. Seeing the&nbsp;success of my students firsthand reaffirmed my belief&nbsp;that the achievement gap is a solvable&nbsp;problem, and I am just as committed, now as I was on day one, to make sure this becomes a reality.</p><p></p><p>I put a significant amount of&nbsp;effort and care into creating lesson plans and curricula&nbsp;tailored to meet my students' needs.
