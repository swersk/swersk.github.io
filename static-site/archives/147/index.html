Sugar Research is Exploding!

<p>“Sugar has adverse health effects above any purported role as ‘empty calories’ promoting obesity. Too much sugar doesn’t just make us fat; it can also make us sick.” -A recently published study* publicizing the negative effects of sugar.</p><p></p><p><a href="http://laurenswersky.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/images.jpg"><img alt="images" src="http://laurenswersky.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/images.jpg" width="259" height="194" /></a></p><p></p><p>Big take-aways from the research:</p><p></p><p>-added sugars are much worse for our health than natural sugars (i.e. processed or prepared foods, sugary drinks, prepared cereals <span style="line-height:1.5;"> are a big no-no; fruit and natural fruit juices are ok). It may seem obvious, but the body of research showing the dangers of sugar is just starting to grow.</span></p><p></p><p>-Recommendations for our daily allotment of sugar varies widely: added sugars should make up somewhere between 10-25% of our diet; we should limit our intake to 100 calories (for women) or 150 calories (for men).</p><p></p><p><span style="line-height:1.5;">-15 countries have a tax for sugary drinks. Will the U.S. follow suit?</span></p><p></p><p><span style="line-height:1.5;">Stay tuned...</span></p><p></p><p>*http://archinte.jamanetwork.com/article.aspx?articleid=1819573</p>
